What is Mehndi/Henna?

Henna is a small shrub called Lawsonia Inermis. It is commonly found in Asia, and Northern parts of Africa. The leaves of this shrub are dried and made into a powder. It is this powder that is used to make the henna paste which dyes the skin.

Mehndi is art of Henna. This is where the henna paste is used to create patterns on the skin. Mehndi is painless and non-permanent. A mehndi pattern can last between 1-3 weeks depending on the individual's body chemistry and after care.

The terms Henna and Mehndi are often used interchangeably.

How long will it last?

On average, the design will look good for 1 to 2 weeks. It will begin fading gradually within that time. Factors that can affect how quickly the design lasts include:
  • depth of the stain
  • body area stained
  • exposure to water, chemicals and rubbing
